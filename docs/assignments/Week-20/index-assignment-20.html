Exercise 2 Here are some relationships expressed in terms of dot products:

  1. The square length \(|\vec{v}|^2\) of a vector \(\vec{v}\) is \(\vec{v} \cdot \vec{v}\).
  2. The cosine of the angle between two vectors \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\) is \[\cos(\theta) = \frac{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}}{|\vec{v}| \ |\vec{w}|}\]
  3. The projection of \(\vec{v}\) onto \(\vec{w}\) is \[\left(\frac{\vec{v}\cdot\vec{w}}{\vec{w}\cdot \vec{w}}\right)\ \vec{w}\]

Here are two vectors:

\[ \vec{v} = \left(\begin{array}{r}3\\-2\\1 \end{array} \right)\ \ \ \ \text{and} \ \ \ \ \vec{w} = \left(\begin{array}{r}2\\2\\-2 \end{array} \right)\] For each of the following, do the arithmetic calculations. Make sure to show your calculations so that it’s obvious which formula you are using.

  1. What is the \(\left|\vec{w}\right|^2\)?

  2. What is the angle between \(\vec{v}\) and \(\vec{w}\)?

  3. What is the scalar \(\gamma\) by which you would multiply \(\vec{w}\) in order to produce a vector \(\gamma \vec{w}\) that approximates \(\vec{v}\) as closely as possible?
